Introduction

If the terms “machine learning” and “crash severity prediction” or similar items are searched on Mendeley’s website (link), we will find that firstly, a large amount of articles have been written in these fields, and secondly, this amount of articles has increased in recent years. I have downloaded and reviewed these articles more or less in recent months and created an Excel file for reference management. Through the investigations, I realized that the articles often focus on a part of the whole data, which I show below with my own division:

  • Location
    • Freeway (Wen et al. 2023; N. Li, Park, and Lambert 2018; J. Li et al. 2020)
    • Highway-rail crossing (Keramati et al. 2020; Kutela et al. 2023)
    • Intersection (Russo, Yu, and Smaglik 2023; Sharafeldin, Farid, and Ksaibati 2022; S. Zhu 2022; Lin and Fan 2021)
    • Workzone (Dimitrijevic, Asadi, and Spasovic 2023; Yu, Zheng, and Ma 2020; Ghasemzadeh and Ahmed 2019)
  • Factor
    • age (Mafi, AbdelRazig, and Doczy 2018; Lee, Guldmann, and Rabenau 2023)
    • Time-of-day (Behnood and Mannering 2019; L. Song, Fan, and Li 2021)
    • alcohol (L. Song, Fan, and Li 2021; Liu and Fan 2020; Lasota et al. 2020; Q. Wu and Zhang 2018; Shyhalla 2014)
    • seat belt (J. M. Kim et al. 2021; Abay, Paleti, and Bhat 2013)
    • at-fault (Rezapour, Nazneen, and Ksaibati 2020)
    • gender (Lee, Guldmann, and Rabenau 2023; Billah, Sharif, and Dessouky 2022; Fu, Lee, and Huang 2021; Mafi, AbdelRazig, and Doczy 2018; Amarasingha and Dissanayake 2014)
    • visibility condition (Harris et al. 2023; N. Li, Park, and Lambert 2018)
    • weather conditions [Naik et al. (2016); Zhai et al. (2019); Yazdani and Nassiri (2021)](Sawtelle et al. 2023; Ghasemzadeh and Ahmed 2019; Zeng et al. 2020)
  • Type
    • car-truck (Zhou et al. 2020; D. Song et al. 2023)
    • cross-median (Lu et al. 2010; Hu and Donnell 2011; Das et al. 2018)
    • fixed object (Holdridge, Shankar, and Ulfarsson 2005; Yan et al. 2022)
    • hazardous material (Shen and Wei 2021; Sun et al. 2022; Xing et al. 2020; Iranitalab, Kang, and Khattak 2018; Ahmed, Gaweesh, and Ahmed 2020)
    • head on (Liu and Fan 2020; Kardar and Davoodi 2020)
    • rear-end (Mohamed, Mohamed, and Al-Harthi 2017; Prajongkha, Kanitpong, and Jensupakarn 2023; Shao et al. 2020; Champahom et al. 2020; Yu, Zheng, and Ma 2020)
    • Rollover (Khan and Vachal 2020; Hu and Donnell 2011; Bullard et al. 2023; Rezapour and Ksaibati 2022)
    • Hit and run (Jiang et al. 2021; Zhou, Li, and Zhang 2018; S. Zhu and Wan 2021; Sivasankaran and Balasubramanian 2022)
    • multi vehicles (Yazdani and Nassiri 2021; Wen et al. 2023; Ma et al. 2023)
    • single vehicle (Ma et al. 2023; Naik et al. 2016; Sivasankaran, Rangam, and Balasubramanian 2021; Roque, Jalayer, and Hasan 2021)
    • red light running crashes (Zhang et al. 2021; Shaaban et al. 2021)
    • roadway departure (Alhasan et al. 2018; Peng, Geedipally, and Lord 2012; Yu, Ma, and Shen 2021)
  • User

    • cyclists (Eriksson, Niska, and Forsman 2022; Boufous et al. 2012)

    • pedestrian (Zhai et al. 2019; Sivasankaran and Balasubramanian 2022; M. Kim, Kho, and Kim 2017; Zafri et al. 2020)

    • motorcycle (Farid and Ksaibati 2021, 2021; Wahab and Jiang 2020; Kitali et al. 2022; Salum et al. 2019; Rezapour, Nazneen, and Ksaibati 2020; Prajongkha, Kanitpong, and Jensupakarn 2023)

    • large truck (J. Li et al. 2020; Azimi et al. 2022, 2020; Behnood and Al-Bdairi 2020; X. Zhu and Srinivasan 2011; Behnood and Mannering 2019; J. Wu et al. 2023; Hosseinzadeh, Moeinaddini, and Ghasemzadeh 2021; Okafor, Adanu, and Jones 2022)

    • teen drivers (Duddu, Kukkapalli, and Pulugurtha 2019; Villavicencio et al. 2022; Hossain, Zhou, and Sun 2023)

    • children and adolescents (Theofilatos, Antoniou, and Yannis 2021; Rezapour and Ksaibati 2021)

In some articles, some combination conditions of the above division are used
